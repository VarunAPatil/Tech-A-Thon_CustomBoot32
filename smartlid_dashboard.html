02 — Root Cause Analysis
Where does temperature control collapse?
Out-of-Bound Temperature Hours — By Supply Chain Stage HIGHER = MORE RISK
Temperature excursions are zero at origin and escalate sharply at air cargo handoff and destination stages — the stages with the least oversight.
At the Point of Care — Passive Cooling Reality
⚡ The Passive Cooling Gap
Field rooms average 22.8°C — a typical Indian ambient. Passive ice carriers can only hold +3°C by the time they reach patients. Every additional minute the carrier is open accelerates this drift.
Temperature Drift Over Time — Safe vs. Discarded Batch PASSIVE COOLING DEGRADES
Batch #002 (Safe, Pune) — maintained near-stable temperatures throughout with active freezer storage.
Batch #003 (Discarded, Chattisgarh) — temperature rose sharply from −68°C to +7°C during local handling. Discarded at 595h remaining expiry.
🌡️
Passive Cooling Cannot Compensate for Ambient Heat
Traditional ice-lined carriers are designed for short journeys. In Indian field conditions averaging 22–25°C, carrier temperature at point of care reached only +3°C — and falls further with every lid opening. Each opening for a single dose exposes the remaining stock to warm air for 30–90 seconds.
📍
No Location Verification at the Critical Delivery Stage
The dataset records zero last-mile GPS coordinates for all 17 discarded batches. There is no way to confirm whether delivery was attempted, which route was taken, or how long the carrier spent at each stop. Without location data, field compliance is entirely unverifiable.
💉
Vaccine Count is Unknown at Point of Administration
Current carriers have no dose-level inventory tracking. There is no way to distinguish between vaccines that were administered, wasted from temperature exposure, or simply unaccounted for. Discrepancies between dispatched and administered doses go undetected until an audit — weeks later.

04 — Our Solution
SmartLid: Active control. Real-time visibility. Zero new waste.
♻️
Retrofit, Don't Replace.
SmartLid replaces only the lid of an existing vaccine carrier. Hospitals keep their current carriers — adding active Peltier cooling, live GPS, dose counting, and a display screen. No new hardware body. No equipment scrap.
Capability Traditional Carrier SmartLid
Temperature Control Passive ice cooling only. Degrades with time and ambient heat. Avg +3°C at field delivery. Active Peltier + fan maintains set temperature regardless of ambient. No ice needed.
Response to Lid Opening Each opening allows warm ambient air in. Temperature recovery is slow and passive. Fan re-circulates cooled air immediately on closing. Recovery is active and measurable.
Location Verification None. Field delivery route and timing are self-reported. No GPS record. Continuous GPS tracking logged to server. Delivery routes and timestamps are verifiable.
Vaccine Count Tracking Manual, paper-based. Discrepancies detected only at audit. Spoilage unmeasured. Automated dose counter records each dose removal. Inventory is always known in real time.
Field Worker Accountability Delivery confirmation is self-reported. No independent verification of visit or dose count. GPS + dose count + time-stamp provide an automatic, tamper-evident field record.
Visibility Dashboard None. Supervisors learn of failures days to weeks after discard. Live screen on device. Real-time alerts if temperature, location, or count anomalies arise.
Hardware Waste Full carrier replaced when damaged or upgraded. Significant material waste. Only lid is replaced. Existing carrier body is retained. Minimal scrap.
Deployment Cost Full unit replacement required across all health centers. Lid-only retrofit. Fraction of full replacement cost. Works with existing inventory.
